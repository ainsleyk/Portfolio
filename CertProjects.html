Undergrauate Certificate Program Projects

All work completed during GIS Certificate program at University of Washington Tacoma. Analysis were completed with ArcMap, ArcGIS Online, ArcScene, ArcCatalog, PowerPoint, and Excel. Images links to site created during the program.

Impervious Surfaces on School Grounds

Creating hypothetical elementary school feeder regions was a great way to explore Thiessen polygons. Each region was used to analyze the amount of natural or paved(impervious) areas these students have access to at school. Zonal Statistics was used to determine the percentage of school grounds where impervious from a raster dataset. There are many factors that influence the amount of natural space on a school campus that are explored here.


Impervious Surfaces



Toxic Soils & Youth

Tacoma housed a copper smelter for over 100 years, this led to contaminates such as arsenic and lead having high concentrations the surrounding soils. With neighborhoods transforming to family communities this puts young children at risk. An interpolation of the test points by the Department of Ecology can provide a raster estimating the concentration over Pierce County. Using the raster calculator, we can identify zones that have a high level of contaminates and are family neighborhoods with young children. Marking these as high priority sites for remediation.

Arsenic & Lead Affected Residental Parcles

Taking a closer look at the smelter pollution, each residential parcel is outlined if arsenic levels are above the state limit of 20 parts per million.

Wealth & Family


The wealth divide can be shown in a variety of different ways, here ArcScene is used to visually show the contrast between high- and low-income neighborhoods in Tacoma. This also examines how wealth looks in areas with many growing families and small children. Using Census data an interpolation was used to find the averages for in each area of Tacoma so they could be visualized in ArcScene.
